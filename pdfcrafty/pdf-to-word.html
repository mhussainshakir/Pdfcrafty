About PDF to Word Conversion

Converting a PDF to Word lets you edit, reformat, or repurpose content that was originally locked in a fixed-layout PDF. PDFCrafty reads the text from your PDF page by page and rebuilds it as a structured Word document, preserving paragraph breaks so the text remains readable and editable.

Because this conversion happens fully in your browser, your document never leaves your device. This is especially useful for resumes, contracts, letters, and reports containing personal or sensitive information.

Note: Since PDFs are designed as fixed visual layouts, complex elements like multi-column text, tables, and embedded images may not convert perfectly. For text-heavy documents, results are typically very usable.

Frequently Asked Questions

Will the converted Word document keep the original formatting?+

PDFCrafty extracts the text content from your PDF and places it into a Word document, paragraph by paragraph. Complex layouts, columns, and graphics may not be preserved exactly as in the original PDF.

Can I convert scanned PDFs to Word?+

Scanned PDFs (image-based) don't contain selectable text, so this tool can't extract text from them directly. You would need an OCR tool first to make the text recognizable.

Is the conversion private?+

Yes. The PDF text is extracted and converted into a Word document entirely in your browser. Your file is never uploaded to a server.

What file format will I get?+

You'll receive a .docx file compatible with Microsoft Word, Google Docs, and LibreOffice Writer.

Is there a limit on PDF size for conversion?+

There's no artificial limit, but very large PDFs depend on your device's processing power since conversion happens entirely in your browser.
