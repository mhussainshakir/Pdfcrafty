About Word to PDF Conversion

PDF is the standard format for sharing documents that need to look the same on every device. Converting your Word document to PDF ensures your resume, report, or assignment displays exactly as intended, without formatting shifting due to different fonts or software versions on the recipient's computer.

PDFCrafty reads your .docx file's content — including headings, paragraphs, bold and italic text, and lists — and renders it into a clean PDF document, all within your browser. Your file is never uploaded anywhere.

Frequently Asked Questions

What Word file formats are supported?+

PDFCrafty supports .docx files (Word 2007 and later). Older .doc files are not currently supported.

Will my formatting be preserved?+

Basic formatting such as headings, bold, italics, lists, and paragraphs is preserved. Highly complex layouts with custom fonts may render slightly differently.

Is converting Word to PDF private?+

Yes. Your document is processed and converted entirely in your browser. It is never uploaded to a server.

Is there a file size limit?+

There's no artificial limit, but very large documents depend on your device's processing power since conversion happens entirely in your browser.

Will the PDF have a watermark?+

No. PDFCrafty never adds watermarks to your converted files.
