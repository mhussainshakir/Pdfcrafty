About Image to PDF Conversion

Converting images to PDF is useful for creating a single shareable document from scanned pages, receipts, certificates, or photos — instead of sending multiple separate image files. PDFCrafty places each image on its own page, in the order you choose, and packages everything into one PDF.

This tool runs entirely in your browser using your device's processing power. Your images are never uploaded to any server, so even private photos remain completely confidential.

Frequently Asked Questions

What image formats are supported?+

PDFCrafty supports JPG, JPEG, PNG, and WEBP image formats.

Can I combine multiple images into one PDF?+

Yes. Upload as many images as you like, drag to reorder them, and they'll all be combined into a single PDF with one image per page.

Can I choose the page size and orientation?+

Yes. You can choose between Fit to Image, A4, and Letter page sizes, and Portrait or Landscape orientation.

Is converting images to PDF private?+

Yes. Your images are combined into a PDF entirely in your browser. Nothing is uploaded to a server.

Is there a limit on the number of images?+

There's no artificial limit, but very large numbers of high-resolution images depend on your device's memory since everything is processed in your browser.
