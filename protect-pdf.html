About PDF Password Protection

Adding a password to a PDF is a simple way to keep sensitive documents — contracts, financial statements, personal records — from being opened by anyone who doesn't know the password. PDFCrafty encrypts your PDF using standard AES encryption that's compatible with Adobe Acrobat and most modern PDF readers.

The entire process happens in your browser. Your file and the password you choose are never sent to any server.

Frequently Asked Questions

What encryption does PDFCrafty use?+

PDFCrafty uses standard AES-128 PDF encryption to password-protect your document, which is compatible with Adobe Acrobat and most PDF readers.

Will my password be sent anywhere?+

No. The password you set is used only locally in your browser to encrypt the PDF. It is never transmitted or stored anywhere.

What happens if I forget the password?+

PDFCrafty cannot recover or reset passwords. Make sure to remember or securely store the password you set, as it cannot be retrieved later.

Is protecting a PDF private?+

Yes. Your file is encrypted entirely in your browser. It is never uploaded to a server.
